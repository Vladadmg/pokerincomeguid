Say there are 3 players in a pot and one has exhausted their chips, you and the other player can continue betting in a side pot. And only you and the other player are entitled to that pot. Even if the other guy wins the main pot, only the two in the side pot play for it.

Side pots come into play when one player has moved all in but there are at least 2 more players left in the hand with chips to continue. So if Bob moves all his chips in, then whatever is in the pot plus his all-in bet is the main pot. The other players Jane and John have more money so can continue betting. Their betting becomes a new side pot. So if at showdown we still have all three players and Bob has the best hand, then Bob can only win the original main pot. The side pot goes to either John or Jane, whoever has the best hand. Bob cannot have a piece of the side pot because he didn't contribute to it. If John had the best hand at showdown then he would take everything.The idea is that it is unfair for someone to win money when they haven't risked a fair amount. So they are limited to what they put into the pot. This is why you want to have a decent sized stack at all times on a cash table, when yo do make the nutz you really want to have the other guy covered to maximise your win. Also, once play is going to move into side pot you generally need to have something to bet. This is because there is one person all-in already and you can't make this person fold because he is already pre-committed and you have to showdown. So any bluff bets are actually into an empty side pot. So bluffing makes little sense. Usually it is quite easy to read the situation once a side pot is established. Sorry havn't had my morning coffee yet but theres the gist of it. The best way to learn is just to play, it will be very clear and easy after a few times.
